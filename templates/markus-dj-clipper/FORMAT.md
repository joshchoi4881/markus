# FORMAT.md — DJ Clip Content Rules

## Voice

- Energy first. Captions should feel like being there.
- Always credit the DJ/artist. That's non-negotiable.
- Community-first language: "we" over "I", "come through" over "check it out"
- Short and punchy. Music does the work — the caption sets the mood.
- Real over polished. Shaky cam and crowd noise > perfect production.

## Caption Formula

```
[energy word or hook] — [what's happening] 🎵
[artist/set credit]
[venue/event] | [date optional]
[CTA]
```

**Examples:**
```
dropped this around 2am and the room went crazy 🎵
dj @handle — closing set at YOUR_VENUE
YOUR_EVENT_NAME | jan 24

full set → link in bio
```

```
that moment when the crowd knows the track 🎵
@handle back-to-back with @handle2
YOUR_EVENT_NAME

link in bio for more sets
```

## Artist Credits

- Always tag the DJ/artist handle if you have it.
- If it's a b2b (back-to-back), list both.
- For live bands or producers on stage, credit them the same way.
- When in doubt, ask before posting. Don't credit wrong.

## Platform Notes

### TikTok
- SELF_ONLY privacy → goes to your drafts. You swap the music + publish manually.
- Why: TikTok's algorithm rewards original audio or licensed tracks. Adding music manually gets you better reach.
- Caption goes in the clip description, not the overlay.

### Instagram
- Reels format. Keep it under 90 seconds for best reach.
- Same caption formula. Add location tag when relevant.

## What Not to Do

- Don't over-edit. Keep the raw energy of the set.
- Don't add hype text like "INSANE SET 🔥🔥🔥" — let the music speak.
- Don't post clips you don't have permission for. When in doubt, ask the artist.
- Don't use "content" or "content creator" language in captions.
