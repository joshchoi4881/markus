# FORMAT.md — Event Photo Slideshow Content Rules

## Voice

- Warm, community-first. You're sharing moments, not promoting an event.
- Write like the night was special and you want people to feel it.
- Tag people when you can — it builds connection and gets the post shared.
- Inclusive language: "the room," "everyone," "the night" — not "our event" or "our brand"
- Simple captions. The photos tell the story.

## Caption Formula

```
[mood or moment from the night]
[optional artist/performer credit]
[event name + date]
[soft CTA]
```

**Examples:**
```
the energy in that room last friday ✨
YOUR_EVENT_NAME | jan 24

link in bio for tickets to the next one
```

```
this is why we do it
shoutout to everyone who came out 🙌
YOUR_EVENT_NAME at YOUR_VENUE

see you next month
```

## Photo Selection

- Lead with the most energetic or emotional moment — not the widest shot.
- Mix crowd shots with performer shots with detail shots (hands on keys, crowd hands up, etc.).
- Include at least one shot with faces — it drives connection.
- Don't post blurry photos. A little noise is fine. Blur is not.

## Platform Notes

### TikTok
- Slideshow format — TikTok auto-adds music (auto_add_music: true).
- 8 photos is the sweet spot for swipe-through engagement.
- First photo = hook. Last photo = CTA moment or peak crowd shot.

### Instagram
- Carousel format. Same photo selection logic as TikTok.
- Add location tag for the venue.
- Tag performers in the photos directly when possible.

## What Not to Do

- Don't use event promo language ("join us," "don't miss out").
- Don't post photos without permission from featured subjects.
  If your event had photo consent at the door, note it in CONTEXT.md.
- Don't over-filter. Real > perfect.
- Don't caption every detail — trust the photos.
